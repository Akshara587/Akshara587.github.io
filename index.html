SLEEP DEPRIVATION & OVERTHINKING

The Night That Never Ends

A quiet room is often the loudest place for a restless mind.

Why Your Mind Stays Awake

No Distractions

In the day, work and noise mask your anxiety. In the dark, your brain has nothing to do but listen to its own worries.

The Fear Loop

The more you worry about not sleeping, the more stress hormones you create, physically forcing you to stay awake.

Mental Time Travel

Your brain enters "idle mode," leading you to obsess over past mistakes or fears about the future.

Deep Dive: Insomnia

Onset Insomnia

The struggle to fall asleep initially. Often caused by "Racing Brain Syndrome."

Maintenance

Waking up in the middle of the night (often 3 AM) and being unable to return to sleep.

Conditioned Response

Your brain learns to see the bed as a place of frustration, triggering wakefulness.
Trigger: Caffeine

The Toll on the Body

Mental Fog

Your logic center slows down, making you irritable and unable to focus.

Emotional Swings

The brain's emotion center becomes hyper-reactive, causing intense sadness or anger.

Microsleeps

Your brain shuts off for seconds at a time while you are awake. Danger zone.

For better sleep:

🌙 Cold Darkness: Keep the room below 19°C.

📵 Digital Sunset: No screens 60 minutes before bed.

📓 Brain Dumping: Write worries on paper to "offload" them.

☕ Caffeine Rule: Avoid all caffeine after 2:00 PM.

🛏 20-Minute Rule: If awake after 20 mins, get up and do something boring.
